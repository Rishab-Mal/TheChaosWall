Comparing data-driven LSTM and physics-informed Hamiltonian Neural Networks on a chaotic double pendulum

Background

Chaos, captured

The double pendulum is deceptively simple — two rods, two masses, gravity — yet it is one of the most studied examples of deterministic chaos. Two trajectories starting an infinitesimal distance apart diverge exponentially, making long-horizon prediction a fundamental challenge for any model.


TheChaosWall pits a pure data-driven approach (LSTM) against a physics-informed approach (Hamiltonian Neural Network) to see how structural inductive biases about energy conservation change prediction accuracy and rollout stability.

500 Simulations generated
~90K Training windows
10 s Trajectory length each
4D State: θ₁ θ₂ ω₁ ω₂

Architecture

Two approaches, one problem

Both models predict the next state of the double pendulum, but they encode very different beliefs about how the world works.

⚡
LSTM

A sequence model that ingests a sliding window of past states and predicts the next state directly. No physics knowledge is baked in; the network learns patterns purely from data. It is fast to train but accumulates error in extended rollouts.

input_size=4 hidden=64 output=4 window=20 data-driven
∮
Hamiltonian Neural Net

Instead of predicting states directly, the HNN learns a scalar energy function H(q, p). Time derivatives are then computed via automatic differentiation of Hamilton's equations — dq/dt = ∂H/∂p, dp/dt = −∂H/∂q — enforcing energy conservation by construction.

learns H(q,p) autograd frozen LSTM encoder physics-informed

Pipeline

From simulation to comparison

STEP 01
Generate Data

500 double pendulum simulations with random initial conditions, integrated with a symplectic implicit midpoint solver and stored as Parquet.

STEP 02
Train LSTM

A sliding window LSTM is trained to map sequences of past states to the next state using MSE loss over the Parquet dataset.

STEP 03
Train HNN

With the LSTM frozen as a state encoder, the HNN head is trained to learn the Hamiltonian energy surface and reproduce derivatives.

STEP 04
Compare

Both models roll out predictions for unseen trajectories. Results are plotted in a 2×2 grid — ground truth vs LSTM vs HNN — to evaluate long-horizon accuracy.
